Before you start

Use a stable chair, clear walking path, and support nearby.

Stop and skip the workout if you feel chest pain, faintness, severe breathlessness, new weakness, or sharp pain.

How to run each check
Timed Up and Go

Sit in a chair. Stand up, walk 3 meters, turn, walk back, and sit down. Use your usual walking aid if needed. Enter the seconds.

Chair stands

Count how many full stands you can do in 30 seconds. Use armrests only if that is your normal safe method.

Balance stage

Pick the hardest stance you can hold safely: feet together, semi-tandem, tandem, or single-leg. Keep support within reach.

Confidence

Choose high only when timing and counting were clear. Choose low if pain, interruptions, or uncertainty affected the result.

How to use this app Daily workout, periodic checks, progress review

Every day

Open Today, review any safety alerts, then complete the Workout plan. Mark the workout done when finished.

Once a week or every few days

Use Assess to refresh the function score. The app uses the latest checks to adjust workout level, support, and progression.

When reviewing progress

Use Progress to see what improved, what still needs attention, and what to focus on next.

Evidence & Sources What this app is based on

Frailty Coach uses validated assessment domains and evidence-informed exercise categories. The 0-100 Function Resilience Score is a wellness coaching composite, not a validated diagnostic frailty score.

Fall-risk screening and functional assessments

CDC STEADI: yearly fall-risk screen, Timed Up and Go, 30-second chair stand, and 4-stage balance test.

Exercise levels and prescription

Vivifrail functional levels and multicomponent exercise framing; USPSTF guidance on exercise interventions for older adults at increased fall risk.
